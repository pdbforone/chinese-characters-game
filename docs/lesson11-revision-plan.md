## Theme & Narrative Structure

### Title

**"The Dog Who Cried Peach"**

### Saga

**Family** — Part of the family and animals narrative arc

### Theme

A remarkable dog discovers a portent: the sky is raining peaches. While villagers scramble for utensils and the oracle cow makes declarations, only the dog remembers what happened before — and prepares for what comes next.

### Memory Palace

**The Peach Rain Village** — A village at the edge of fields where sweet fuzzy peaches fall from the sky like rain. The journey moves from the initial portent → through the peach rain → the dog's remarkable silence → the villagers' reactions → the cow's declaration → the dog's preparation.

### Callback

Dog (狗) returns as a crazy companion in Lesson 12

### Narrative Arc

_"A remarkable dog discovered a portent: the sky was raining peaches."_

Portent appears → Peaches fall → Pooch sees first → Remarkable observation → Silence held → Sort of thing unprecedented → Crying begins → Utensils grabbed → Stinking dog avoided → Other dogs flee → Villagers detest → Pressure mounts → Cow steps forward → Special oracle speaks → Declaration made → Vast change proclaimed → Before remembered → Washing prepares

## Official Narrative Story

From `lesson_stories_prose.md`:

> It began with a **PORTENT (兆)** — cracks in the sky, whispers in the wind.
>
> Then came the **PEACHES (桃)** — falling like sweet, fuzzy rain.
>
> The **POOCH (犬)** saw it first. He was **REMARKABLE (尤)** — sharper than other dogs.
>
> He sat in **SILENCE (默)** for a long time, watching the fruit fall.
>
> This was a **SORT OF THING (然)** that had never happened before.
>
> Then he began **TO CRY (哭)** — not from sadness, but from the weight of knowing.
>
> The villagers grabbed every **UTENSIL (器)** they could find to catch the peaches.
>
> But the dog was **STINKING (臭)** — he'd rolled in something foul that morning.
>
> The other **DOGS (狗)** avoided him. Even the peaches seemed to fall elsewhere.
>
> The villagers grew **TO DETEST (厭)** him. His smell. His crying. His knowing.
>
> **PRESSURE (壓)** mounted. Someone had to explain this miracle.
>
> The **COW (牛)** stepped forward. She was **SPECIAL (特)** — the village oracle.
>
> She began **TO DECLARE (告)**: "This is a sign! A **VAST (浩)** change is coming!"
>
> Everyone listened. Everyone except the dog.
>
> He remembered what came **BEFORE (先)** — the last time peaches fell.
>
> So he went **TO WASH (洗)** himself in the river, preparing for what was next.
>
> _The cow could declare. The dog would prepare._

## Character Flow & Groupings

### Act I: The Omen (Portent & Peaches)

1. **兆** (zhào) - portent — "It began with a portent — cracks in the sky, whispers in the wind"
2. **桃** (táo) - peach — "Then came the peaches — falling like sweet, fuzzy rain"

### Act II: The Observer (Dog's Witness)

3. **犬** (quǎn) - pooch — "The pooch saw it first"
4. **尤** (yóu) - remarkable — "He was remarkable — sharper than other dogs"
5. **默** (mò) - silence — "He sat in silence for a long time, watching the fruit fall"
6. **然** (rán) - sort of thing — "This was a sort of thing that had never happened before"

### Act III: The Response (Crying & Catching)

7. **哭** (kū) - to cry — "Then he began to cry — not from sadness, but from the weight of knowing"
8. **器** (qì) - utensil — "The villagers grabbed every utensil they could find to catch the peaches"

### Act IV: The Outcast (Rejection)

9. **臭** (chòu) - stinking — "But the dog was stinking — he'd rolled in something foul"
10. **狗** (gǒu) - dog — "The other dogs avoided him"
11. **厭** (yàn) - to detest — "The villagers grew to detest him"
12. **壓** (yā) - pressure — "Pressure mounted. Someone had to explain this miracle"

### Act V: The Oracle (Cow's Declaration)

13. **牛** (niú) - cow — "The cow stepped forward"
14. **特** (tè) - special — "She was special — the village oracle"
15. **告** (gào) - to declare — "She began to declare"
16. **浩** (hào) - vast — "A vast change is coming!"

### Act VI: The Preparation (Memory & Washing)

17. **先** (xiān) - before — "He remembered what came before — the last time peaches fell"
18. **洗** (xǐ) - to wash — "So he went to wash himself in the river, preparing for what was next"
