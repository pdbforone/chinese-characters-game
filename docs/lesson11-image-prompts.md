## Act I: The Omen (Portent & Peaches)

### 1. 兆 (zhào) - portent (Tone 4: COMMAND)

```
A contemporary Neo-Gongbi painting on sized silk depicting the moment cracks appear across a twilight sky above a peaceful village.

The sky fractures like ice breaking, jagged lines spreading outward from a central point. Below, villagers pause their work, faces upturned in sudden alarm. The cracks glow faintly with otherworldly light, each fissure a message from the heavens. Wind carries whispers no one can quite hear.

The composition arranges the spreading crack lines to subtly evoke the Chinese character 兆, as if the sky itself wrote the word "portent" in its breaking.

Rendered with fine-line gongbi technique, pale azurite sky with stark dark fissures, cinnabar glow at crack edges, visible silk texture. Sharp decisive energy of cosmic announcement. No visible text.
```

### 2. 桃 (táo) - peach (Tone 2: GASP)

```

Fuzzy pink-orange fruits tumble through the air in impossible abundance. A peach tree stands at the center, but the fruit comes from everywhere — from the broken sky itself. Villagers look up with open mouths, hands reaching. Each peach carries the mark of the omen, sweet messengers from beyond.

The composition arranges the tree trunk with portent-like branches to subtly evoke the Chinese character 桃, as if the shape of peach was written by nature's own hand.

Rendered with fine-line gongbi technique, warm cinnabar and coral for peaches against malachite leaves, upward-lifting surprised energy, visible silk texture. Rising wonder at miraculous fruit. No visible text.
```
