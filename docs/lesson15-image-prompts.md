# Lesson 15: The Police Poet — Neo-Gongbi Image Prompts

## Lesson Overview
- **Title:** The Police Poet
- **Theme:** Officer Chen's terrible poetry is the most feared weapon in the precinct
- **Memory Palace:** The Police Precinct
- **Characters:** 22

## Visual Style Notes
- Police precinct atmosphere with institutional blues and grays
- Paper, books, and written documents as recurring motifs
- Contrast between seriousness of law enforcement and absurdity of poetry weapons
- Sound waves and speech bubbles for spoken word characters
- Prison bars and official seals where appropriate
- Characters should be visible and integrated naturally into scenes

---

## Character Image Prompts

### 1. 書 (shū) — Book
**Tone 1 (SING) — Sustained operatic warning**

In the style of Neo-Gongbi with meticulous brushwork and luminous washes: A worn leather-bound poetry book resting on a police officer's desk, its pages faintly glowing with dangerous verses. The character 書 is formed by the gold-embossed title on the spine. Steam rises from a coffee cup nearby, curling into musical notes that hang in the air. The desk lamp casts dramatic shadows across official police paperwork. Cool institutional blues contrast with the warm golden glow of the book. Atmosphere of quiet menace, as if the book itself holds power.

Aspect ratio: 4:5, vertical composition emphasizing the book's presence.
